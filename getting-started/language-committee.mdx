## Overview

The Language Committee serves as the cornerstone of Heritage Lab's commitment to linguistic accuracy and cultural authenticity, providing essential guidance on all aspects of the Ai! platform's language development to ensure our technology truly serves communities.

## Purpose

The Language Committee provides essential guidance on linguistic accuracy and cultural authenticity across all Heritage Lab language technology development:

- **Linguistic Guidance**: Provides guidance on dialectical variations across regions
- **Content Review**: Reviews and approves language content before AI implementation
- **Quality Standards**: Establishes standards for linguistic quality and cultural appropriateness
- **Grammar and Implementation**: Advises on grammar rules and implementation strategies
- **Community Representation**: Ensures community voices are central to decision-making

## Committee Structure

### Inuktitut Language Committee

The Inuktitut Language Committee is composed of Inuit language experts and community members who provide guidance on all aspects of Inuktitut language technology development.

**Current Committee Members:**
Lisa Mesher, Natasha Ita MacDonald, Thomassie Mangiok, Harriet Keleutak, Eva Ilisituk, Tukirqi Pilurtuut, Jeannie Nayoumealuk, and Shaun Annanack

### Key Advisory Areas

The Committee guides critical decisions about:

- **Dialectal Representation**: How dialectal variations should be represented in AI systems
- **Linguistic Resources**: Which linguistic resources and grammar rules should be prioritized
- **Cultural Appropriateness**: Cultural appropriateness of generated content
- **Translation Standards**: Translation standards and approaches
- **Specialized Terminology**: Methods for handling specialized terminology

### Committee Responsibilities

- Attend bi-monthly meetings (six meetings annually)
- Review and provide input on language projects
- Guide policy development
- Represent community interests and values
- Review and approve language materials and tools before AI implementation

## Decision-Making Process

### Consensus-Based

- The committee strives for consensus in decision-making
- All members have equal voice in discussions
- Decisions respect community protocols and values

### Meeting Structure

- **Regular meetings**: Bi-monthly meetings (six meetings annually)
- **Duration**: Approximately 3-4 hours per meeting
- **Format**: Primarily virtual with at least one in-person gathering annually
- **Additional work**: Paid review work available as needed between meetings

### Documentation

- Meeting minutes are maintained and shared with committee members
- Decisions are documented and communicated to relevant stakeholders
- Records are maintained according to our data retention policy

## Compensation and Recognition

### Honoraria

- **Full-day meetings**: $300 honorarium
- **Half-day or shorter meetings**: $150 honorarium
- **Additional review work**: $75/hour for work outside of meetings
- **Travel expenses**: Travel and accommodation expenses covered for in-person meetings

### Recognition and Impact

Committee members are acknowledged for their contributions to the Ai! initiative. Your expertise directly shapes technology that preserves language for future generations, creating digital tools that respect dialectal diversity and cultural authenticity.

## Community Engagement

### Representation

- Committee membership reflects the diversity of partner communities
- Efforts are made to ensure geographic and linguistic representation
- Community members are supported in their participation

### Communication

- Committee activities are communicated to partner communities
- Communities can provide input through committee members or direct communication
- Regular updates are provided on committee decisions and activities
